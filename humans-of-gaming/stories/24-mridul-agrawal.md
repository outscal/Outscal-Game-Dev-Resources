Meet Mridul Agrawal

A guy who went to the future. (or at least his vision did)

Story of a middle-class boy from NCR who left his ‘safe’ and ‘sturdy’ job for an ‘unsafe’ job. Job security is a highly subjective concept but let's save this for some other day.

A different angle to look at Mridul’s decision would be to see how Mridul shifted from a slow-paced environment to a fast-growing environment.

A long conversation with him strengthened my belief in the fact that ‘what IT was for millennials, gaming will be for GenZ’.

Most of us (millennials) became the first generation tech professionals within our families because of the global IT boom. As we grew up, we saw this tech industry evolve and diversify into new deeper technologies.

GenZ now will dominantly become the first-generation drivers of these deep technologies. Mridul understood this after working for 2 years in the IT industry.

Mridul developed a passion for game programming in the second year of his college. He started building his first game project after completing a course on Unity and parallelly, he scored a job at Tata Consultancy Services in campus placements.

He quoted, “There were days when I had two laptops opened in front of me. One to wrap up my office work and another to learn game development and build game projects. I analyzed the contrasting yet resembling nature of both work experiences and decided that I liked one experience a lot more than the other one.”

He found out about Outscal and joined the Full-stack Game Development program after he was sure that he wanted to build a career in the gaming industry.

Mridul left his job and started focusing full-time on his program with full commitment.

It took him less than 6 months to transition to the gaming industry. Mridul joined Dirtcube Interactive at a very early stage and has been closely working with the founders Mikhail Bhuta.

# Rahul Bhansali

**Senior Engineering Manager · Zynga**

> An entrepreneur by heart who worked on two failed startups but made a successful career out of it.

---

Rejection is redirection.

Even in times of despair, we have faith, which helps us get through. I have heard that often but it became even more evident to me after I met Rahul Bhansali.

Rahul’s journey began with an early internship at Cilantro Technologies in Jan 2011 as he was eager to learn and grow as an engineer. It was his sheer dedication that this internship was converted into a full-time job within a few months. It was a place where he learned to take ownership, plan and implemented everything.

Rahul left his job and started building his own travel-tech venture ‘Gidangel’ with a couple of friends. To be able to carry this product to its full term it was necessary for each one of the three founders to come together or else they would have to abort the product altogether. But since it wasn’t on cards for all to come together they had to halt the development of the product midway.

He worked on Airpush for 2 years before he joined as a first employee in a bootstrap food-tech startup that came into the market at the time of Swiggy. This startup could have changed his career trajectory but it failed as it was early to the market.

Trying to focus on himself and know better in terms of his career choice, he decided to take a break so as to know better whether a startup or a job was his final calling.

Being a worthy candidate he had three offers on the table while hunting for a job. He chose the company which was comparatively offering lesser remuneration because he wanted a new challenge and got started in game programming.

Dream company for many, Rahul was now in Zynga. Having previously worked in food tech, ad tech, and various product-based companies, gaming was going to be a new reign and he was up for the challenge.

As he looks back he is grateful for the opportunity and even more glad that he got to explore the field of gaming.

He had often heard from people that gaming is a hard and complex field. So he began to give us all and toiled hard to know more about this field and excel in the same and soon with his efforts in a time span of seven years he was promoted multiple times from a Jr. engineer to an engineer manager and further senior manager.

He is proud of the decision he made to join the field of gaming as it gave him a chance to work on Pictionary games, casino games, match-3, and infinite runner which he feels is a good opportunity to not only learn but to grow in the field as well.

Rahul’s journey is full of re-directions as we saw with the start-up not coming through etc but he did not get disappointed and continued on his journey to learn and explore something new and strive to make a mark in his own life.

His career journey is a clear example of how rejections do not mean a halt but rather a redirection and a blessing in disguise.

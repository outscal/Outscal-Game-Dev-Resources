"Career in games is a joke!”

This is exactly what Swarup’s relatives told him when he decided to pursue game development.

Ft. Swarup Mohanty

The best part of the story is that Swarup had full support from his parents to follow his passion, who didn’t give a sh*t about societal opinion.

Since childhood, he was passionate about playing football. He tried playing football professionally and was a robust goalkeeper in his high school football club.

He even participated in All India Championship but unfortunately, he couldn’t pursue a career in football due to multiple reasons.

He decided to try his other interest focused on games.

He says “ I have loved playing puzzle games since my childhood. They intrigued me to think about how developers made them.

When I graduated from High School, I joined Backstage pass to pursue a career in game development.

My relatives went all crazy wondering what I was doing with my career. I played Yakuza 0 for the first time during my college and it completely blew my mind.

I fell in love with Japanese art and storytelling. I dreamt of working for a similar game project.

I found myself fond of coding during my college days and started decoding a few games. I noticed Gotham Knight had a weak implementation of enemy path finding. I rewrote the code for the same (how I would have done it).”

6 months into Outscal’s game development program and now he’s is presently at “Gameshastra” and loving his experience there.

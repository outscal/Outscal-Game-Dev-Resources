“I knew I wanted to become a game developer since I was 15.”

Featuring Tanya Gupta from our Women in Games.

” I learned to code for the sake of becoming a game dev, my teacher told me to learn JAVA and C in order to build games."

Tanya spent a few months learning C only to realize that it was a little complex to build games with C.

Based out of Bareilly, she pursued BCA from Bareilly College only to keep her interest in programming alive.

When COVID happened, Tanya got access to Unity’s official live sessions. ” Those live sessions were amazing but were not meant for beginners”

“One fine day, while scrolling through LinkedIn, I came across the Launchpad 🚀. A cohort-based program to learn game development and I felt that was all I needed.”

“Prior to this, I tried to learn on Udemy, Youtube, and various self-paced courses. I learned high-level stuff like making the character move and all but never understood how all of it was happening.”

” Launchpad made me understand those concepts in depth. Concepts like how to move the characters. What are components and how you can access properties inside it.”

This is one of many fundamental and advanced-level things that our folks learn in our full-stack game development program.

Tanya is one of the most active members of the community.

She’s also a super moderator ☑️ helping us manage our fast-growing community.

A big inspiration to many in the community.

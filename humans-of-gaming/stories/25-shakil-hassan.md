“I came across a book called Rich Dad Poor Dad when I started college. Robert Kiyosaki talked a lot about building assets and reducing time trade for money. This stuck to my head forever.”

Featuring Shakil Hassan.

Coming from a remote village of Assam, Shakil went to Shillong for his bachelor's degree. He wanted to learn coding, so he opted for B.tech.

In his own words he says “I got admission in B.tech Biomedical Engineering which wasn’t all about coding. There were a few coding languages in the syllabus and other subjects were inclined towards medical sciences and biology. I failed in all subjects except coding.”

Shakil failed the semester and wanted to try again but his fate had different plans. His mother left the world and his family was going through a financial crunch. He had to drop his college degree and go back to his village. He then decided to stay at home and continue learning programming.

He wanted to buy an online course on coding but didn’t have Rs. 25000 to invest in it.

Later, he started doing BCA from a local college in Assam and went to a friend's place who had a computer to practice his learnings.

When asked, he said “I learned various ways to earn money from coding. For example, I bought the source code of a game from the internet, made some changes to the code, created a new skin, and then published it on Play Store.

Did it a few times and one of my games went viral. It got 100K downloads and I earned Rs. 80,000 by investing Rs.1500 and a few hours in it. This was when Robert Kiyosaki’s quotes hit me again! I had built an asset which was giving me recurring income”

Shakil decided to pursue a full-time career in game development and came across “Outscal’s game development program.”

He recalled, “I had saved Rs.10,000 for my dream trip to Leh-Ladakh. Satnamji Arora from Outscal called me and told me that the security deposit to reserve the seat was Rs.10,000. I sacrificed one dream for another and joined Outscal.”

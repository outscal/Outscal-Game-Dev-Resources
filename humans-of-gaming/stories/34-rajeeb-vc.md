Declined the first offer from Wipro when he had no job. Declined another from EA Australia when he was at Zynga.

Meet [Rajeeb VC](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAFACQgB5SsHKTU76_y_sIpfyYGyEMPk3Pk) – The first game developer in Zynga India.

Rajeeb has been a computer freak since high school. He was into programming and won multiple prizes in coding competitions. He went for a degree in electronics engineering to keep his career options open.

Besides coding, he liked games. Not only playing but building them as well. Before work, he programmed everything from a Minesweeper to Solitaire and Spider Solitaire-themed games.

He took up every game bundled with Windows back in the day and tried to rebuild it. He was also working on a Monopoly game as a side project.

Despite having a degree in electronics and getting a VLSI engineer position from [Wipro](https://www.linkedin.com/company/wipro/), he rejected the offer and joined a small company named SupportSoft as an associate. This was Mark Pincus’ second startup before establishing Zynga in 2007.

Rajeeb spent four years here working in technical support products. This was where he met [Shan Kadavil](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAACQry0BRQtf5gkxzN9bXxywB4eQls-X-NA) as his manager and mentor.

Zynga India was founded in 2010. Shan led the studio, and Rajeeb followed his lead. Rajeeb being a hardcore gamer was a big fan of Zynga’s titles like FarmVille and Mafia Wars, and some of the biggest games of Zynga (till today). He got an offer from Zynga and was ecstatic with the new position

Rajeeb joined [Zynga](https://www.linkedin.com/company/zynga/) as one of the first programmers.

Zynga India was handling live ops for a couple of big titles like Farmville, and Cityville. Rajeeb was the only in-house game dev at the India office, He contributed to nearly every game that came up to India Studio.

Initially, he handled localization support and bug fixes for games like Special Forces, Street Racing, Football Wars, Fashion Wars, Dragon Wars, Word Scramble, and more. Later on, as the studio expanded, Rajeeb transitioned to a managerial role.

At this time, he was working on Mafia Wars along with [Suraj Nalin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAUm9jIBy8QzE6qs85VJV_LX22U9i261epw) play [Felix Manojh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAOBwe0BByQyQqZzyvLZ-ven-P3Nzhp49OQ), and [Kumar Puspesh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAFPoPcBi6w9a_BDyNjgfuQiBF3GBS__9ho). Interestingly, most of his initial colleagues at Zynga — the first programmers are now running big game dev companies.

[Puspesh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAFPoPcBi6w9a_BDyNjgfuQiBF3GBS__9ho) established [Moonfrog Labs](https://www.linkedin.com/company/moonfrog-labs/), which developed games like Teen Patti Gold and Bahubali.

[Suraj](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAUm9jIBy8QzE6qs85VJV_LX22U9i261epw) set up [PlaySimple Games](https://www.linkedin.com/company/playsimple-games/) in 2014 — where Rajeeb would later go on to work. Felix founded [FLIXY GAMES](https://www.linkedin.com/company/flixy-games/) and went on to join [PlaySimple Games](https://www.linkedin.com/company/playsimple-games/).

Rajeeb joined in as one of the first members of the studio. When he left in 2019, it was one of the biggest Zynga offices in the world. A decade-long journey filled all the ups and downs.

His next challenge was at PlaySimple — as the Director of engineering.

The company was building multiple games on Unity. When Rajeeb joined, the company just had one game based on the engine. He built a team and expanded the number to four games.

Soon after this, the pandemic happened, He was surprised at how the market changed and saw an opportunity.

Rajeeb described this shift as the third major revolution in gaming.

After the shift from consoles and computer games to web games, web gaming to mobile gaming, the shift into Web3 felt like the next big thing.

Thus, he started venturing into starting a game dev studio. He started Circle of Games with a primary focus on casual and hyper-casual games for the Indian market.

The company’s building multi-gaming, pay to earn platform on the blockchain. The games on COG platform reward players in COG tokens, a cryptocurrency that will launch soon.

Rajeeb is working with the tech of the future — Web3 games will soon take over the market. Circle of Games has a clear head start.

Throughout his career, Rajeeb has constantly chased his passion and kept pushing for the better. He never had a second thought about a career in gaming.

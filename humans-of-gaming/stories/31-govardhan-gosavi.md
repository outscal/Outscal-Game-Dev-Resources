# Govardhan Gosavi

**Game Director / Co-founder · Xigma Games**

> “While my classmates were focused on IIT-JEE, I was busy playing Counter-Strike." Back in high school, Govardhan knew that engineering or medicine wasn’t for him.

---

A 23-year-old failed game developer who went on to develop the hit indie title The Bonfire, showcased at IGDC, Tokyo, and Singapore indie game showcase.

“While my classmates were focused on IIT-JEE, I was busy playing Counter-Strike.

Back in high school, Govardhan knew that engineering or medicine wasn’t for him.

Gaming cafe culture was at its prime, and Govardhan and his friends would go to play there in the evenings all day. In his 12th, he played Max Payne and got hooked.

While his classmates were preparing for JEE and NEET, he was playing Counter-Strike all day.

He, however, wasn’t sure that a career in game dev is possible.

Govardhan, however, was good in programming and art. His parents asked him to search for a suitable career.

Having spent months searching for opportunities, he stumbled across game dev studios and colleges like DSK Sup Info, Pune, and ICAT, Bangalore.

He ended up joining ICAT to learn game development.

Govardhan recalls that he was taught everything from art to design and programming.

However, there was no clear focus on programming.

He got huge industry exposure and participated in multiple competitions.

In one such competition, Dare To Be Digital, hosted by a Scottish university, Govardhan and his team was the only one selected from India. He even won a prize for the best graphics in a video game.

Govardhan also met developers of games like Crisis and GTA there.

He later applied for an internship position at RZ2 Games and was working on Tribal Trouble 2. He was scripting and designing the quests for the game there.

After completing his degree, he continued working as a full-time employee at [RZ2 CO](https://www.linkedin.com/company/rz2co/).

He worked on Play Jackpot Sports, an online betting game, and eventually became the Project Lead of Virtual Casino, a 3D casino made using Unity.

However, all of this time, he was trying to set up his own game dev studio. but couldn’t get through with it. In 2013, he quit RZ2 in pursuit of developing a successful studio.

He tried setting up Manaburn Game Studio with a friend but failed.

He spent over two years at Manaburn Game Studios building multiple mobile games.

After that, he quit, and through a friend’s reference, got into a Chinese game dev studio, Moon Dragon Technology.

# Egor Golsky-Dias

**Game Design Mentor · Freelancer**

> How a guy with an education in advertisement made into the biggest game studios in Russia?

---

The journey started more than two decades ago when Egor graduated from a college in Moscow with a degree in Advertisement. He started working in the industry and soon got frustrated with what he was doing.

Took a step back and started reflecting on his interests, As he loved playing games, he got introduced to Game Maker, and RPG Maker and started learning them.

Stars aligned and one of his friends referred him to a game studio that was looking for a tester role. This was Egor’s first step into the gaming industry.

This company would later get acquired by Glu Mobile, giving him exposure to big IPs like Call of Duty, Guitar Hero, Monsters Vs Aliens, and companies like DreamWorks Animation, and Activision.

“I had no educational qualification to break into games, It all came with experience, intuition, and hard work,” mentions Egor.

Egor was offered the role of level designer after working as a tester for almost 2 years. The transition changed everything for the rest of his career.

He would on to work with OSMO GAMES where he worked on two unreleased titles and Game Garden where he was a game designer on a City Builder game by Disney.

Up next, was Russia’s giant Mail.ru Group where he was working on an AAA MMO-RPG game Skyforge. He was working in the combat design team where he was scripting enemy behavior, creating patterns, and coming up with Ideas.

Egor recalls, “He was working on an internal tool which roughly resembled unreal blueprints”

He took a sabbatical after spending more than 7 years in the industry.

During the break, he started working in a toy-making company which kept him away from the screen and got him into more physical tasks.

Egor couldn’t stay away from building games too long, he made a stringer comeback after 18 months and this time he started working on casual games at Playrix and then switched to 2RealLife to work in MMO-PRG games.

This is where he would work on meta and story aspects of the game like characters, locations, quests, dialogues, and story progress.

Egor mentioned, “People around me don't know what I do for a living, but they are fascinated by the industry where I work in.”

Egor is currently consulting gaming studios and giving back to the community by mentoring people who’re trying to break into the gaming industry.

# Yadu Rajiv

**Lead Game Designer · PlaySimple Games**

> What started with Game Boy, Sega Mega, and Dendy, grew into a passion for game development that never stopped.

---

Yadu got access to his elder brother's computer at a very young age where he started learning Flash, and QBASIC programming.

He found his brother’s book on visual basics which intrigued him more about coding. Yadu started learning C, C++, and design tools like Photoshop.

Coming from a small town in Kerala, he had to move out to look out for more opportunities in games. Yadu did not have anyone to guide him through a career path in the gaming industry, but he knew he wanted to tell stories through games. He shifted to Delhi where he thought of joining MAAC to learn animation. He started freelancing to support his living in Delhi.

A branch of Arena Animation had just opened up in Trivandrum and his previous boss referred him to that branch. Yadu started as an instructor in Arena Animation in 2007 while freelancing as a game developer on the side. After two years he switched to a UX designer role at OSS Cube.

Yadu always wanted to build a community that could have helped people come together, interact, and network. That was the start of gamedev.in.

Up next, Yadu started building MyyHashstash Studios as his first venture, trying to figure out how the startup is done. Parallelly, he started pursuing a master's from the National Institute of Design.

He soon got an offer from redBus to join as Sr. UX designer.

After spending a year at Redbus, he joined the Srishti Manipal Institute of Art, Design, and Technology to fulfill his love for art, design, and tech.

He wanted to bring a course in game programming there which felt way ahead of time. he hosted an Indie game festival back then where games like Asura, Super Hot, and a bunch of interesting unreleased games were showcased.

After teaching, Yadu really wanted to go back to building games and started interviewing again. He finally got a job at BYJU'S’s as a Game Designer where he built a lot of phygital learning games.

Byju’s acquired a game studio called Tangible Play based out of Palo Alto. Yadu closely worked with their team to ship games. Having spent two years at Byju’s.

He got a call from PlaySimple Games on a weekend. Sat on a call with the Director of design, and got an offer by Wednesday.

Yadu Rajiv is a self-taught game developer who navigated the early days of the internet with a dial-up connection and central cafes to find resources for his passion. He explored what was possible with Flash and taught himself programming, which later helped him pave a career in games. Yadu's journey shows that with a clear vision and hard work, you can turn your passion into a career.

“I was a born genius, education ruined me.”

Meet Komal Kurundwade: A sketch artist turning into a game programmer

Komal learnt all the basics of computers before she was 10. She skipped the normal 11th & 12th standard and directly pursued a 3 year diploma in CS after completing her 10th grade.

Running way ahead from other kids in her generation, one thing killed her fast growth ⚡

A 4-year B.tech college degree.

Based out of a small town called “Ichalkaranji” in Maharashtra, she knew a lot more things about Computer Science than her college mates.

Unfortunately, many tier 3 colleges in India never demand creativity or encourage students to innovate. They expect students to learn theories and write them as it is in their exams.

The Covid lockdowns gave people extra time to explore their interests.

Komal started doing various courses on Coursera during this time. She completed a couple of courses but realized that she kept forgetting what she was learning as it failed to keep her intrigued and engaged.

As an avid mobile gamer, she challenged herself to learn game programming. This is when she found out about Outscal and joined our full-stack game development program.

Her enthusiasm is also shared by her father, she said “My father is a farmer and he wants me and my siblings to learn and do whatever we wish to, he’s been a constant support all these years.”

# Huzaifa Arab

**Co-founder, Creative Director & CTO · Hypernova Interactive**

---

“I was denied admission to engineering because of poor marks in Chemistry”.

A BBA graduate currently building the biggest mobile game in India.

Everyone in Huzaifa’s family was academics — he was the only kid who wasn’t good at studies. 📖

Having an IITian elder brother made things different. 🏫

His brother would bring him demos of games, which he would play on repeat for hours. Huzaifa being the creative kid, tried to mod those demos after completing them.

His dad taught him to code at the very early age of 7. 👩‍💻 This is how he got into game dev “without even realizing” it.

After his 12th, Huzaifa was denied admission to engineering. He had poor marks in Chemistry. Thus, he had to go for a BBA.

He met [Mayur Bhimjiyani](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAZ0OycB6-hgj7BYIRyeQb9ztxyKE8lQDoM) in a gaming cafe, with whom he would later set up [Hypernova Interactive](https://www.linkedin.com/company/hypernova-interactive/). Mayur and Huzaifa used to go around India playing e-sports tournaments.

One of these tournaments was hosted in [DSK International Campus](https://www.linkedin.com/company/dsk-isd-international-school-of-design/)— the best game dev school in India back then.🎓

When Huzaifa went to the campus, he knew this was it.

He was confident this was what he needed for his game dev career. 🎮

Huzaifa was at a turning point — should he go for an MBA or pursue game dev?

He, however, decided to take the leap of faith. After completing BBA, Mayur, and Huzaifa rented two computers. Buying them was out of their budget. For four months, they learned modeling and polished their programming skills. 👩‍💻

They applied to DSK Sup Info with their updated profiles and both of them got in.

Both Huzaifa and Mayur recall the network they built at DSK has immensely helped them.

Huzaifa recalls that the college taught them team management and introduced them to work experience at game dev companies.

At college, Huzaifa was also working as a freelancer.

He developed an entire game for a client for which he got 1 lakh rupees. It was dirt cheap for the client, but it was a lot of money for a student.

In his second year, as Mayur went on for an internship at Zynga India, Huzaifa joined Kreeda Games as a game designer. The concept of game design was still relatively new, so he had to struggle to learn.

Huzaifa learned a ton from this position — managing a company and running it efficiently with few resources. These would later help him at Hypernova.

After completing their degree from DSK, Mayur joined Zynga and Hufaiza received an offer from Hike Messenger’s game dev team, a studio that was just getting set up.

Huzaifa learned a lot at the company. According to him, Hike was the first company that “ran with the professionalism and resources of an enterprise but had the flexibility and speed of a startup.”

He developed tons of games in teams of five built into the Hike Messenger. He worked on a cricket matching game, a runner game, Bollywood Ka Baap, and Shiva the Time Bender.

Hike would conduct accurate market research to build games that reached more people. They would go door to door asking people about their likes. Hufaiza was at the center of all this and kept learning heaps about the Indian market.

Later, when he was setting up Hypernova with Mayur, this mentality helped him direct the company in the right direction.

Huzaifa spent over two years at the company.

One day, he and Mayur were chatting about the new iPad in the market and Huzaifa said it would be fun to play Dota on it. But, Dota is only available on PCs and no mobile equivalent existed back then.

Huzaifa knew this is what he had to work on.

In the meantime, Hike was also changing a lot of directions. So it only was logical for him to quit his job and take the leap of faith. He knew if he didn’t take the initiative, he would regret it forever.

In May 2015, Mayur and Huzaifa founded Hypernova in Bangalore.

The focus was to always build a product-first company but eventually, Hypernova had to offer service-based work to survive.

The first game they built was War Tanks which launched back in 2016 and it got over a million downloads.

Right now, Hypernova is working on Mayanagri — the biggest mobile game from India. It is an open-world gangster game set to launch soon.

Mayanagri has created ripples ever since it was announced. The game packs stunning visuals and graphics.

[Huzaifa](https://www.linkedin.com/in/arabhuzaifa/)’s journey has been one of vision and passion — he chased his love for video games and utilized every opportunity to grow.

He had a ton of difficulties, but he persisted and overcome them.

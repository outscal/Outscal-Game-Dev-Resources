# Mayur Bhimjiyani

**Co Founder & Studio Head · Hypernova Interactive**

Zynga India’s first Intern is now building India’s first open-world mobile game. The game is the talk of the town - Mayanagri

Featuring Mayur Bhimjiyani

The Story will run in parallel with his co-founder Huzaifa’s journey.

Back in the early 2000s, the gaming café culture was at its prime. Gamers would play for hours sitting in dark gaming cafes.

This is the same time when Mayur was completing his degree in commerce. Coming from a small business family, his parents wanted him to become a Chartered Accountant.

Mayur on the other hand was spending most of his days playing e-sport games in cafes. This is where he would meet Huzaifa, a small-town boy who looked at gaming from a different lens.

Mayur’s vision was fixed on making it big as an e-sports player. He was eyeing an e-sports tournament which was conducted in 2009 at DSK International Campus.

Huzaifa decided to tag along for the competition. Mayur’s team would go on to win the first prize in the competition and a sum of Rs. 10000/-

Huzaifa in a parallel universe was more happy to be on the DSK Campus. He realized that he had landed in the only institute in India that could train him to break into the gaming industry. This would go on to change everything. By this time, Mayur was done with his B com degree and Huzaifa was done with his degree in BBA.

Both were now determined to join DSK Sup Info and soon convinced their parents to start their degree in Game Design!

A year at this completely changed how they looked at their career. Mayur was learning fundamental and advanced concepts of game programming from industry professionals.

Mayur had his eyes set on the biggest AAA studios in India back then — EA and Zynga. Zynga didn’t have internship opportunities then but this didn’t stop Mayur. He kept cold-calling HR until the studio organized an interview for him.

Mayur convinced Zynga by showcasing his skills — he was onboarded as a design intern. At the studio, Mayur worked firsthand on games like Vampire Wars.

He designed a new turn-based combat system for the game and added a few years to its shelf life and ran the game on his own for about four months.

After reviewing his performance, Zynga was ready to offer Mayur a full-time position.

But, he denied it. Mayur wanted to go back to DSK and finish his final year first. In his second year, Mayur was assigned a project — to develop a game. With a team of five, he led the development of MoonCube. Mayur joined back Zynga and would go on to spend the next 3 years there where he would go on to lead the first team of game designers at Zynga India.

He worked on flagships games like Farmville, CastleVille, and more…

It was 2015 now, Mayur was done with his job and now wanted to build something of his own. He put down his papers at Zynga and his partner in crime tagged along.

Huzaifa Arab left his job from Hike and they were ready to build their first bootstrap indie studio - Hypernova Interactive.

The studio that started with a vision to build the first action RPG game on mobile in 2015 would go on a roller coaster ride to build India’s first Open world mobile game a few years later.

The journey started by building an alternative to DOTA and LOL on mobile, in 2015 (5 years before they launched it for mobile). The plans drifted away as Hypernova failed to raise capital.

‘We have quite a few prototypes of mobile games that are lying around in my dusty games library, but we haven't released any of them. We pivoted, did some other stuff, made a couple of games, got featured internationally by Google and Apple, but eventually ran out of money.’

Left with two choices, Either shut the shop or stick to it and dive into the service industry. ”We were very clear from Day 1 that we would stick to our own products and never do services, but had to do it for the survival of the company.”

It worked out well, the next three-year service operations would give them the confidence to build their own game.

2020, An internal game hackathon gave birth to the Idea of a first-person shooter game called ‘MumBhai’. The gaming landscape was different now, LOL mobile was out, DOTA mobile was out, and PUBG was killing it with numbers in India.

Jio changed how Indians used the internet, and UPI revolutionized the micro transaction behavior.

Mayur and Huzaifa dug deeper into the idea and decided to go ‘all hands in’ on a new open-world game, curated for India.

Birth of ‘Mayanagari’

Mayur’s journey in gaming is a testimony of how persistence and perseverance can change everything for you.

He started as a hardcore gamer and his passion for games is what drove him for these many years.

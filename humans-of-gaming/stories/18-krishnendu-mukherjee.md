# Krishnendu Mukherjee

**Co-Founder · Giga Fun Studios**

> The journey from PWC, Wipro Consulting, and KPMG to gaming studio.

---

A school kid who was determined to get into a good engineering college from his high school days.

Krish got into Jadavpur University in 2004 as he had planned and started pursuing degree in electronics and communications engineering.

3 years into it and he got a taste of consulting during the third year of his college while interning at PwC

“12 years ago, I didn’t even know that a career in games was a thing. For me, games existed magically.”

Krish’s internship at PWC turned into a full-time job where he was consulting and had a client-facing role. About 3 years later, he decided to pursue MBA and went to ISB. He joined Wipro after completing his master's and year later switched into KPMG.

One thing that got Krish into gaming was ‘traveling during work”. He hated it and wanted a job that kept him in one place.

Krishnendu’s entry into gaming is as accidental as it can get. He went to a beer club in Bangalore where he met the producer of Mafia Wars.

Krish himself was a big-time Mafia Wars player and was blown by the fact that its development team was in Bangalore, few miles away from him.

Next, he reached out to Harish Kundapur at Zynga

”I wrote this big a** covering letter and I sent it to all sorts of possibilities of Zynga email IDs, and it landed in his inbox.”

3 years in Zynga and he grew from Associate Product Manager to Lead Product Manager.

In between, he switched to Moonfrog Labs (founded by Ex- Zynga) and came back to Zynga.

Upnext, He spent a year at GSN Games as Director of Product Management where he was handling Social casino games.

He then tried a brief stint at a Fintech company which didn’t workout, so he decided to switch back to gaming.

Krish left his job, the moment the world was hit with the pandemic.

Gaming picked up during lockdowns and so did Real Money Gaming.

Krish had multiple incoming offers and chose to go to MPL.

He was closely working with Sai Srinivas Kiran G for more than a year and during this time he had figured out that he wanted to either start something of his own or figure out a way of making a living without working for someone else.

“So I decided to quit MPL. And then I was at home. I was tinkering, drawing, painting, playing, coding.”

Parallelly, Rahul Daga (now co-founder of Giga Fun Studios) had put down his papers and was ready to start his entrepreneurial journey.

Gigafun happened next!

“Games have helped me at times when I felt like an imposter with certain things.”

Krishnendu switched to gaming after spending 5 years outside games. Even though resetting one's career can be quite challenging, It isn’t impossible if you have passion for it.

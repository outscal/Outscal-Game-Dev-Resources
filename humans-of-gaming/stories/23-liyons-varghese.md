You’re looking at a game developer who doesn’t like playing games.

I am not saying he hates games but he’s not all crazy about games.

Featuring Liyons Varghese

I have shared the journey of many game programmers so far and a majority of them were avid gamers before they started building games.

The inspiration to build games came while playing games for them.

This story is different, Liyons never had a device to play games. He didn’t have a high-end gaming laptop or access to a PlayStation. He got his first laptop when he joined college.

He opted for IT in his high school where he got introduced to C++ and later chose computer science in his college. Clueless of what he was doing in the first year, he started hands-on coding in the second year of his degree.

His close friend Milan was an early member of the Outscal Community. Milan shared a game development playlist with him which intrigued Liyons about building games. Liyons started learning game programming in the 3rd year of his college degree and joined Outscal in the final year.

” Building games now gives me immense satisfaction. I skipped my college placements because I was confident about getting a job placement from Outscal’s Game Development program.”

Loving games is one thing and building games is another. You may or may not be a hardcore gamer, but the passion to build games is not dependent on it. In my college days, I was a hardcore gamer. Now, ironically, I don’t remember the password of my Steam account.

Liyons will become an inspiration for those who think only gamers can become game developers.

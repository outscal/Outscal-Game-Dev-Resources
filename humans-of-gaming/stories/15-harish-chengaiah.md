# Harish Chengaiah

**Founder, Game Director, Writer & Producer · Outlier Games**

> An 8th-grade boy who decided to become a game designer and never looked back.

---

His passion for game design started when he was just 14 years old. It was the year 2008-09, and Harish had just finished playing a game when the credits started to roll. As he went through the credits, he noticed different job titles such as programmer, artist, QA tester, producer, and designer.

As he researched more about each of these job roles, he found that the designer's job was the perfect fit for his creative sensibilities and problem-solving skills. He decided then and there that he wanted to become a game designer.

From that point on, Harish's education focused on honing his game-designing skills. By the time he got to college, he already had a good grasp of game development and design. He spent countless hours self-learning through GDC videos and game design books.

Unfortunately, the college he went to was useless, and he dropped out after the second year.

For Harish, making a calculated decision that is useful for him was more important than taking the safe route. He didn't want to waste time and money on courses that wouldn't add any value to his dream of becoming a game designer. He was a commerce student and had several options such as BBA, BCom, or BA literature. But he felt that these courses wouldn't help him become a better game designer. He would instead use that time to hone his skills as a game designer.

Harish's journey started when he was just six years old, thinking he would become a doctor. But as he got older, he went back and forth between different professions such as geologist, forensic scientist, and scientist. However, he was bad at math, so science was out of the picture. So he thought about doing commerce or computer science, but long before he made that decision, he already knew that he wanted to become a game designer.

Harish started nagging his family about his dream of becoming a game designer in the eighth grade. By the time he was in the twelfth grade, they were fed up with it. However, his persistence paid off to convince his parents.

After several failed attempts, the hyper-casual trend caught his attention. In 2017, this trend started becoming popular, and he saw an opportunity.

He decided to make a hyper-casual game and began to brainstorm ideas. Harish had started his first venture called Lazus Studio where he was heavily experimenting with different genres of games, trying to build prototypes of games he thought might work. Eventually, the team disbanded and he decided to move to back to Chennai.

He started writing a game treatment for his next project titled ‘Heroes Project’. After writing the 10-pager, he started working on its GDD. Up next, he came up with an 80-pager script.

This would eventually become the script of the game “Deliverance” which they’re building today at Outlier Games.

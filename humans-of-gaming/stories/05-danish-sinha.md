The story of a 16 YO Bitcoin miner who solely paved his way into the gaming industry.

Featuring Danish Sinha **#HumansOfGaming**

12 year an old fan of Age of Empires.

He clearly knew what he wanted to do. Soon, he joined an engineering college.

The first year of college life made him realize that he’ll have to figure out his career path on his own. College education was bringing no value.

He started pursuing online courses and specialization for international universities.

Started with game programming, and he did 30+ certifications and specializations in fields like AI, ML, Blockchain, Model Thinking, Data Science and realized that he loved building games the most.

Circled down to game programming, he started building games in Python during his college days. By the end of his four-year college degree, he had 3 degrees! One from the University of Maryland in entrepreneurship, One from Michigan state university in Game Design, and lastly the one he was pursuing from Shiv Nadar University. In 2016, He started pursuing his 4th degree from the California Institute of Art in Game Art and Design.

While at college, he was interning at a game studio that was building a military-based game for Switch. He learned how games were being developed professionally which made him even more sure about building a career in games.

He knew that the gaming industry in India was very fresh and the opportunities were fewer. Jobs predominantly existed in RMG and Danish wanted to go into hard-core and mid-core games.

The entrepreneurial DNA in him motivated him to start his own indie studio. This gave birth to Game Stacy!

A 5.0 pointer in college was now building his own game company for which he would successfully raise funds, qualify for Google’s accelerator program and scale the team of 2 to 20.

Danish’s knack for self-learning and passion for games helped him pave his way into the gaming industry. His deep love for technology always kept him ahead of everyone else around him.

A college-going kid who mined 14 BTC when everyone thought it was a fad is now up to something big! Our eyes are on him and his team at Gamestacy!

Wishing him all the luck in the world!

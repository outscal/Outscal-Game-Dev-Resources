# Ganesh Hande

**Co-Founder · nCore Games**

> The guy behind FAU-G, India’s biggest mobile game to date.
>
> Ganesh didn’t begin gaming until he went to college when he wore his dad down to get him a computer. Every computer science student should have a computer — was his version.

---

Ganesh didn’t begin gaming until he went to college when he wore his dad down to get him a computer. Every computer science student should have a computer — was his version.

He managed to get hold of a “free” copy of FIFA and would play the game daily. Initially, he sucked at it.

He used to think he was playing against a real player while it was just the computer’s AI.

Slowly, he got better at the game and could beat the AI. He bragged that he was a pro at the game to his friends, only for one of them to break his illusion.

Ganesh wasn’t playing against another guy — it was the computer!

He was amazed at the technology. He knew he had to do something with games.

After FIFA, he played games like Mortal Kombat, League of Legends, etc. and got into gaming groups and was totally hooked on it.

By the time he was about to finish his engineering degree, he knew he would build his career in the creative arts.

But fate took him in other directions.

The great financial recession of 2008 soon hit India and the jobs market declined. He was working as a sales agent: driving his motorcycles to construction sites and trying to sell some elevators.

One day, Ganesh and company were casually chatting when a friend who had just got a job told him about this company — [Digital Chocolate](https://www.linkedin.com/company/digital-chocolate/) — where they’d build games.

Ganesh was ecstatic. This would be his dream job!

He went to a walk-in interview. He remembers hundreds had come there because of the recession and massive unemployment.

People tried to climb up the boundary walls just to give the interview.

Luckily, Ganesh got in.

Initially, he worked as a QA engineer but soon got into game design.

He was working on Facebook, Android, and iOS games.

About this time, Symbion, another mobile OS also came out. He was designing games for them too.

Most of the games were puzzles, casual, and arcade-styled. The major titles he worked on are Millionaire City, Zombie Lane, Army Attack, for Crazy Penguin Wars for over five years.

In 2013, he was promoted to the position of Product Manager.

After a mere 11 months, [Digital Chocolate](https://www.linkedin.com/company/digital-chocolate/) was acquired by [RockYou](https://www.linkedin.com/company/rockyou/) He maintained his position as the Product Manager in the new company.

Rock You’s business model was different, as Ganesh recalls.

The company would buy IPs and run live ops (maintenance, new updates, etc.)

Ganesh, being an experienced dev, contributed to setting up the new studio in Bangalore. Rock You was working with developers like [Kabam](https://www.linkedin.com/company/kabamgames/) and [The Walt Disney Company](https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-walt-disney-company/).

Ganesh, thus, worked on casual and casino games like Kingdoms of Camelot, Godfather, Dragons of Atlantis, and GREE's War of Nations for Kabam.

He also worked on Disney games like Zoo and Words of Wonder.

However, the industry was slowing down after some time in 2017-18.

Soon there weren’t as many IPs left for Rock You to acquire.

Around this time, Ganesh decided to start something of his own.

In 2018, after getting his MBA, he took the leap of faith.

Ganesh started his own company: [nCORE Games](https://www.linkedin.com/company/ncore-games/)

The founding principles were the same as Rock You — provide live ops service. He bootstrapped the company by landing a gig at [Rovio Entertainment Corporation](https://www.linkedin.com/company/rovio/).

They worked on some features of the classic Angry Birds game and did some live ops for it.

After this, they also worked on other Rovio games.

In 2020, nCORE announced FAU-G.

FAU-G created ripples in the Indian mobile gaming community.

To this date, the game has crossed over 5 million downloads on Play Store alone.

FAU-G has defined the mobile game dev potential of India and paved the path for future games to follow.

Currently, [nCORE Games](https://www.linkedin.com/company/ncore-games/) is working on an ICC cricket game for mobile.

Throughout his career, [Ganesh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ganesh-hande-77a08821/) followed his heart and his passion.

From knowing nothing about games to creating the most influential mobile game in India — his journey is nerve-wracking.

# Roberto Schiavulli

**Co-Founder & Creative Director · ChamoGames**

> "My mother still says to everybody that I work with computers”

---

Who went from reviewing games to working for companies like Riot Games and Activision and finally starting his studio.

Roberto started gaming at a very early age. Initially, he would play video games and write reviews on them. But, he never thought he would build a career in the field.

After university, he worked for two years as an Editor at Press X.it, working on video game reviews. In 2008, he went to a conference about Xbox games in Italy where he met some developers from the industry.

By this time, he was frustrated with reviewing games and he wanted a more active role. There, a guy suggested him to look for a QA role because he had some experience reviewing them. The gaming industry in Italy wasn’t as big back in 2008. Thus, Roberto had to move out of the country and learn English.

After some digging, he was able to land a Localisation QA Tester position at Vivendi Universal Games on a short 6-month contract in Dublin, Ireland.

Roberto recalls that he didn’t particularly know a ton about Localisation QA. However, the company took him in because he was bringing his language skills (Italian) and trained him for the position.

After his contract ended, he joined Warelords for a short time before Activision hired him.

In 2010, he transitioned to a management position as the Localisation Floor Lead.

Activision was a structured company — as Roberto recalls. It follows a strict trajectory while developing any game. He worked as a QA tester there for over 3 years before he joined Riot Games as a Player Support Specialist in 2012.

For a second time, Roberto didn’t know much about the position. However, he learned player support from scratch, did some research, and delivered cool stuff. He recalls that Riot was much different from Activision — the studio focused on quality rather than quantity. Roberto described the feeling like everyone around him felt so smart.

He slowly started to get intrigued by event management. He pulled out some manuals and started reading everything about the process — in Olympic Games, at automobile events, and more.

Thus, when he helped manage the League of Legends launch event, Riot officials were impressed with his work. Gradually, he transitioned to an event manager position at Riot.

Roberto traveled around the world to countries like Poland, the Netherlands, and the UK, going to events like Comic-Con and meeting with multiple industry leaders.

After handling events, he was up for a new challenge as he slowly transitioned to the role of a producer at Riot.

Roberto recalls that the role of a producer and an event manager is largely the same, thus, he didn’t have difficulties in the new position.

Finally, in 2020, his almost eight-year-long stint at Riot Games came to an end. When he quit,

When he quit, he had rose to the position of Product Manager.

Roberto moved back to Italy in 2020 and joined Behavior Interactive — a much smaller studio known for its work ethic and environment. He was still in Product Management.

Behavior might be one of the best studios for anyone to work for, according to him.

However, Roberto didn’t stick to his role and quickly transitioned to the position of a Game Director at Kamamuta Games in Lucca, Italy in September 2020. He had fallen in love with Lucca back when he visited it while at Riot and wanted to move there.

In April 2021, he also joined Dark Slope Studios, a VR-based studio, as a Senior Producer in a remote role. The studio was working on a crossover between League of Legends and Beatsaber.

Roberto transitioned to a lead role a few months later and dramatically improved the quality of work. He handled everything from management to hiring for over a year. He made sure the game made sense from both a gameplay and a business point of view.

After a one-and-a-half-year-long stint at Kamamuta, in December 2020, Roberto took the leap of faith.

He had worked in multiple aspects of the industry for over fourteen years and knew it was time to start his company to assume full control.

In May 2022, Roberto quit his job at Kamamuta and Dark Slope and started working on his startup.

He is working as the Creative Director at ChamoGames. The company is secretive about what it's working on but Roberto expects that the reveal later this year will make some noise in the industry.

Roberto’s career proves that skill and curiosity will take devs a long way — he joined the industry when he knew very little and slowly mastered every skill necessary to make it big.

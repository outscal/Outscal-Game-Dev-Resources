# James C.

**Co-Founder · Stealth Mode**

> From Logistics Manager at Pfizer to working on world’s best gaming IPs and Studios. Featuring James C.

---

From Logistics Manager at [Pfizer](https://www.linkedin.com/company/pfizer/) to working on world’s best gaming IPs and Studios. Featuring [James C.](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAVsJYYB5Bw41H6UP4A5t6e5xI3VjMUIyJg) [#HumansOfGaming](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/hashtag/?keywords=humansofgaming&highlightedUpdateUrns=urn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7052656393153093633)

Basically from the UK, [James](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAVsJYYB5Bw41H6UP4A5t6e5xI3VjMUIyJg) wanted to migrate to Finland, but getting a job in Finland wasn’t a cakewalk for a foreign professional.

He soon got an opportunity from [Supercell](https://www.linkedin.com/company/supercell/). Supercell was still young and had just 80 employees in Finland.

He took the job and started working as Player Support Specialist.

The role was a 360-degree flip from what he was doing earlier. Coming from a white-collar job to a fast-paced gaming studio felt like a paradigm shift.

He recalled how his team at Pfizer took 6 months to make a small decision and in contrast, decision-making was on steroids at Supercell.

“We would literally come up with ideas in the first half and go live with it on production in the second half. The creative freedom we got there was infinite.”

He took the customer support role because that was the first offer that came his way, which completely changed his life.

He was working on Hay Day and loved the culture of the company. He left Supercell because he got offered a role at [Grand Cru Ltd](https://www.linkedin.com/company/grand-cru-games/) and he fell in love with the game which they were building.

“I knew that leaving Super Cell was a crazy move but I did it for my love and passion for the game. I still say that it's probably my favorite game that I've ever worked on. Good enough to make me leave Supercell. The game didn’t do well and a year later, I switched to [Next Games, A Netflix Game Studio](https://www.linkedin.com/company/next-games/).”

Next games already had the IP of The Walking Dead when James joined, He worked on The walking dead: No Man’s Land where he managed their community and hosted events.

He joined in as the 22nd employee and the company blew up to 100 employees in a couple of months.

Having gained 6 years of experience in gaming, James soon started building his own studio.

After consulting various companies, and working on a lot of different games, he started figuring out how to make a successful game company.

Looking for opportunities that nobody else would be looking at and trying to figure out a space where the audience already existed. He circled down to ‘Merge’ games (a Subgenre of Puzzle games) and [Skunkworks](https://www.linkedin.com/company/skunkworks-oy/) was born.

He spent three years building [Skunkworks](https://www.linkedin.com/company/skunkworks-oy/) which generated more than €3m in revenues and raised more than €5m over multiple investment rounds.

Grew the team to 30 people, but had to, unfortunately, shut down because things went downhill.

James’ grind at Skunkworks is what motivated him to start building his next game studio.

Currently, in stealth mode, we’re looking forward to what he’s up to next! Wishing him all the luck and prosperity. ✨

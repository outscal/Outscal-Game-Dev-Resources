# David Helgason

**Founder · Unity Technologies**

---

An aspiring poet and writer studying biochemistry took a loan to afford a trip to California.

He was always passionate about technology and creative writing, and as such, held a keen interest in video games, where both disciplines intersected.

Helgason met Joachim Ante at a demoscene party, an event where programmers, artists, and musicians showcase their skill in creating digital art and music. They both shared a love for video games and the idea of creating something innovative in this space.

This shared passion led Helgason, Ante, and Nicholas Francis to start working on an ambitious project: a sci-fi MMO game named "GooBall."

The project advanced and they realized that the tools they were using were not meeting their needs.

This was the seed that grew into Unity. They began creating their own tools that would later evolve into the Unity game engine.

Unity engine was ready and they decided to showcase it at the Apple Worldwide Developers Conference in 2005.

There was one problem: they couldn't afford the trip to California

They applied for a business loan. And when that wasn't enough, they maxed out their credit cards to cover the remaining cost.

Their booth at the conference was a simple table with a couple of laptops, but it caught the attention of many attendees. Soon, Unity began gaining traction as an accessible, flexible, and powerful game development engine.

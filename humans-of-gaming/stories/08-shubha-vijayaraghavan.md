# Shubha Vijayaraghavan

**Director and Co-founder · Longbow Games Pvt. Ltd.**

> From spending 10 years at Infosys to building a bootstrapped game studio.

---

A software engineer turned game developer who has worked with companies like Rovio Entertainment Corporation, and DECA Games and is working on bringing the game dev scene in India into the spotlight.

Starting from her high school days, Shubha was exceptionally good at math and knew she wanted to pursue Computer Science as a major in college.

Her interest in programming led to her position at Infosys where she worked as a software engineer handling the ins and outs of the software development life cycle.

Over the course of the next few years, Shubha rose the ranks to become a Lead Software Engineer.

She worked for multiple clients and had interesting projects back to back. She flew to different parts of the world, like Atlanta, GA, USA, and Stanford, CT, USA, while working on these client projects.

Over the course of years, she rose to the ranks of technology analyst and finally led a team of engineers in metadata management projects.

Her stint in Infosys is exceptionally long — she worked at the company for over ten years.

However, in 2018, she decided to shift into the gaming industry and joined Treasure Hunt in Berlin. Shubha recalls that the work wasn’t much different: she was still working with data. But the domain and the work traditions in Germany were way different than what she was used to at Infosys.

Shubha’s stint in Treasure Hunt wasn’t long — she came back to her hometown Mysuru in 2019 and started a game dev company, Longbow Games Pvt. LTD., to focus on smartphone game dev.

Longbow’s story is rather interesting. Shubha and her team bootstrapped the company — they didn’t have any initial investments and, thus, have been profitable throughout.

The initial projects were client work for other developers on the market. The first project the company handled was for Wargaming. Shubha quickly went on to land big clients like Rovio Entertainment Corporation, DECA Games, and Kabam.

After gaining some experience in the industry, Longbow is currently working on its own casual video game for smartphones. Shubha expects the title to hit the market by this year.

Shubha’s story is an example of how anyone with a strong will and passion can break into the gaming industry.

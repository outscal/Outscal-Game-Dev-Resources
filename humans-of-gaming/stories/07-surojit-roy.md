# Surojit Roy

**Head of Studio · CrazyLabs India**

> From winding down the studio to building a globally viral game with millions of downloads

---

Surojit started gaming in 1995, he had a gaming PC that brought his interest to the industry.

Cafe culture was on the rise, and Surojit would spend hours playing in cafes with his friends. He would go on e-sport tournaments with friends which led to his interest in hosting them.

He traveled around the country to cities like Hyderabad and Pune competing in Dota All-Stars with his friends. He even made it to some international tournaments.

However, Surojit wasn’t satisfied with the esports industry in India. Thus, in 2010, he bootstrapped AFK Gaming, one of the first online esports gaming communities in the country.

With no prior knowledge of code, art, or designing games, his interests were more aligned with gamers. He understood what gamers looked for in a game. This, however, couldn’t stop him from venturing into the developer side.

Surojit interviewed for the creative writer position at Western Outdoor Interactive (WOI) - A GEE Company, a company based out of Mumbai, and through his sheer knowledge of games, got the position building games for airlines. In the interview, Surojit showed that he knew what a good game is.

However, handling a job, post-graduate studies and AFK Gaming became too much for him and Surojit ultimately quit the esports company in 2013.

Surojit recalls that he didn’t know how to create game design documents back when he joined WOI.

He didn’t have any experience in the field and was trying to figure out how the game development industry works.

Around 2014, Candy Crush Saga was launched. The game rose to the top and was making $3M-5M per day. Surojit was baffled by the numbers and wondered why was he developing games for airlines when Android and iOS are booming platforms.

Thus, in May 2014, he quit his job to build BYOF Studios.

The company focused on building games for the mobile platform. It also offered consulting services and helped other studios with progressing the work.

After working in the company for two years, Surojit felt burnt out. He was working for 16 hours a day without a break to keep BYOF Studios afloat.

Thus, he went into a career break to rethink his decisions and figure out his next move.

During this break, Surojit met Karan, another game developer. Destiny played its magic, as he lived 5 minutes from Surojit’s home.

Surojit and Karan started collaborating and sharing the creative process. This was the most crucial six months of his career, as he recalls.

Later on, Surojit went to start up Firescore Interactive with Karan. But, things weren’t going smoothly.

Surojit recalls that the company was on the verge of shutting down - he didn’t have the money to pay salaries.

To keep Firescore Interactive alive for a couple more months, Surojit wrote a cold mail to CrazyLabs which led to a partnership.

Now with only few shots left, Firescore Interactive started working on the Soap Cutting game. A few months into development, Soap Cutting was ready to be launched in December 2019.

They launched the game on Christmas Eve and it soon became the number 1 app on Android beating Spotify, YouTube, and Instagram.

Surojit and Karan were living the moment they dreamt for years. The overnight success that they got was in build for more than a decade.

This one hit secured the future of Fire Score and solidified their relations with CrazyLabs.

After working on some more hit hypercasual mobile games, Surojit eventually went on for a merger with CrazyLabs so that he didn’t have to worry about every nitty-gritty of keeping the company afloat.

Since September 2021, he is working as the Head of the Studio of CrazyLabs.

The company has developed multiple top hypercasual games and has gathered over 3.5 billion downloads so far.

Surojit’s career is proof that hard work and passion are king in game dev.

# Kumaresh Bhuyan

**Program Manager · Yipli**

> “I got four promotions in three years.” Meet Kumaresh Bhuyan, whose passion for cricket and game dev helped him make it big in the industry.

---

“I got four promotions in three years.” Meet [Kumaresh Bhuyan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAACz5EscBlgoRq_4BeDKg3Y4HVOF4e5Px8Gs), whose passion for cricket and game dev helped him make it big in the industry. [#HumansOfGaming](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/hashtag/?keywords=humansofgaming&highlightedUpdateUrns=urn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7054449084635504640)

[Kumaresh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAACz5EscBlgoRq_4BeDKg3Y4HVOF4e5Px8Gs) was introduced to gaming at an early age in 7th standard. He used to be a cricket fanatic, which helped kickstart his career in gaming later on.

In his final years of high school, he used to play games like CS: GO, Cricket 2007, and GTA: Vice City regularly on weekends at a local café for a couple of hours. However, he never believed that one could make it big by developing games in India.

After schooling, he went on to pursue B. Tech majoring in Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) from a tier 3 college.

At college, one of his friends introduced him to console gaming. He tried out every major release from 2010-15 firsthand. During these years, he developed a deep passion for gaming.

Kumaresh got placed in big companies after graduating. However, he decided to not enter the corporate world and pursue a career in gaming instead.

He did a couple of courses in software and visited cities like Bengaluru and Hyderabad to develop a network. Then, with the help of a recommendation from one of his cousins, he joined a gaming company working on a cricket game as a QA intern.

On the first day at his job, the CTO asked Kumaresh to go through the game and find out some issues. Thanks to his deep knowledge of cricket, which he had developed back in school, Kumaresh listed out some 200-300 problems in an Excel sheet. Both the CEO and the CTO of the company were impressed.

After completing his six-month-long internship, Kumaresh faced the same dilemma that most aspiring game devs face in India. His parents wanted him to get a ‘stable job’.

Most of his friends had followed a similar path and were working in MNCs.

Consequently, he joined the marketing team of the [State Bank of India](https://www.linkedin.com/company/state-bank-of-india/) as his parents demanded.

Kumaresh recalls that he is the only guy in his batch who decided to pursue game development as a career.

The work experience and environment were wildly different at SBI. After a nine-month-long stint in the bank, he quit and joined [All Star Games (Deftouch)](https://www.linkedin.com/company/allstargames/) as a QA Engineer.

He couldn't stay away from games. His deep passion and willingness to learn led him from the position of QA Lead to Junior Project Manager, and finally to, Associate Project Manager.

He was promoted four times within three years. However, soon, he felt like taking up a different challenge and started exploring other opportunities.

His next position was in Fitmat Smart Solutions, a company that works on both games and software, thereby mixing and matching Kumaresh’s varied interests. Currently, he is working on [K{r}eeda Labs | Yipli](https://www.linkedin.com/company/kreedalabs/) , a service that combines gaming and fitness.

Kumaresh’s career path is proof that interest and passion in almost any field can be leveraged to grow in the gaming industry.

# Oliver Jones

**Co-Founder & CEO · Bombay Play**

> From selling newspapers at 13, working at a gas station at 16, to building million-dollar gaming studios from scratch

---

Growing up in Wales and studying in London, as a kid, he made games using magnet mazes and soon started editing levels for Lemmings in DOS.

“I used to build small games and give them to my parents to play”

It wasn't until later that he realized game design was a profession and that he could pursue a full-time career in it. He started out with small applications in Visual Basic and moved on to early versions of game-making tools like RPG Maker and Game Maker.

“My first passion project was a two-dimensional version of Quidditch from the Harry Potter books. It taught me what bad game design looks like and led to my lifelong pursuit of becoming a professional game designer. After graduating from Brunel University's game design program, I landed my first job at the State of Play Games.”

After working for a couple of months there, Oliver got a call from Zynga.

The hottest company in Silicon Valley had called and offered him a job.

Big opportunities come with big problems.

Here’s the catch: The offer came from Zynga India, not the UK office.

The guy who was cycling his way to work in London was prepping to dodge Bangalore traffic now. ”I thought India would be fun, I would travel around, have fun and maybe come back home in a year or two.”

After spending 3 years at Zynga, Oliver thought of starting his own company here.

Moonfrog came into existence!

“Multiple times, we ran into situations when we knew that if we don’t raise money now, we would be dead in water. We walked into Sequoia Capital’s office with a weird PPT and came out with Million Dollars.

After building various games which failed, someone suggested we build a ‘Teen Patti’ game.

A team of 4 built it in 2 weeks. We launched it, It caught virality and we saw 20,000+ downloads overnight.”

This was Moonfrog’s ticket to their Series A round with Tiger Global.

They raised $15 Million on a 15 min call with Tiger Global.

After spending 3 years in Moon Frog, he decided to move on and started building Bombay Play with Abhas.

Mobile gaming was no more the hot chic of high school.

Investors were prudent about their investment in gaming studios.

“We bootstrapped our way to the first million-dollar game. I, Abhas, and Prasanna were working out of a basement of a coworking space in Bengaluru, the cheapest office space we could find, and came out with million dollar game.

I felt vindicated, like, I'd really proven that it's not just luck. There is some skill involved here. And not just anyone can go into the basement and come out with a million dollars gig.”

Justin Waldron (Founder of Zynga) led Bombay Play’s funding round.

Oliver is a living example of a founder who jumps off the plane without a parachute and builds it on their way down before hitting the ground.

From him cycling to work and kids chasing him in the street of London to building a million dollar in the basement of an Indian city, Oliver grew up to be an inspiration for the gaming community.

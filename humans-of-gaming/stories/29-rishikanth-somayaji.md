# Rishikanth Somayaji

**Product Manager · Joyride Games**

---

Rishikanth was into games from early on. He would go to gaming cafes and play Counter-Strike for hours.

He took Commerce after his 10th because he didn’t want to be in the same class that his father taught.

But he realized Commerce wasn’t something that interested him.

Instead, he developed a keen interest in programming and animation, leading him to pursue a degree in the latter.

Excelling effortlessly in 3D software, modeling, and animation, he cultivated his skills further in Premiere Pro and After Effects, even creating animated films for YouTube.

After graduating in 2010, Rishikanth transitioned into freelance infographics editing.

He hated his job.

It wasn't until 2011, when a friend introduced him to [Dumadu Games](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAbbOi4BJpn-1JQdr9OnM9fadRnanLRk8m8), a gaming company, that he began to see a path in the gaming industry.

Initially joining as a marketing team member, his role evolved over time as he displayed a knack for game development, ultimately contributing to Dumadu's development process.

In 2014, the company ventured into esports events, with Rishikanth planning and executing these events.

The Indian esports scene was still nascent.

Hosting these events was a Herculean task. As a result, the events met with limited success.

Around this time, Rishikanth embarked on a personal project: developing Jalebi, India's first multilingual game.

Despite being a simple word-based ludo game, Jalebi was a monumental success, with over a million downloads in a few months and accolades including Google Play Editor's Choice.

At one of the VGF events, he met a Korean publishing company ,[Funizen Solutions Pvt. Ltd.](https://www.linkedin.com/company/funizen-solutions-pvt-ltd/), who were trying to bring Korean mobile games to India.

In 2016, [Rishikanth](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ACoAAAS13CABCrGkEndLi-6Y3gHcNbI9OxGznNo) joined them.

Despite its initial plan to bring Korean mobile games to India, Funizen soon closed its Bengaluru studio.

Rishikanth switched to [Mech Mocha](https://www.linkedin.com/company/mech-mocha/)-- a mobile game dev studio, where he worked on Jetpack Joyride and Hello Play, an online video game platform.

Mech Mocha’s entire team was acquired by [Flipkart](https://www.linkedin.com/company/flipkart/) around this time.The company’s focus wasn’t completely on making fun games, however.

He felt like quitting because it was getting hard to continue working on the game, leading him to [Mobile Premier League (MPL)](https://www.linkedin.com/company/mobile-premier-league/) in 2021.

There, he focused on games like snooker, pool, and carrom, before transitioning to Mayhem, MPL's sister studio, to work on Underworld Gang Wars, a battle royale game.

Rishikanth transitioned to [Joyride Games](https://www.linkedin.com/company/joyride-games-inc/) in late 2022, where he thrives today as a Product Manager.

His 15-year journey in the gaming industry is a testament to his unwavering passion and dedication to the gaming scene.

A teenager who cracked [Burger King](https://www.linkedin.com/company/burger-king/)’s coupon code algorithm.

Launched a website to create unlimited free sandwich coupon codes for visitors programmatically, Ft. Justin Waldron

This teenager co-founded a billion-dollar gaming studio at age of 19.

Zynga

Justin dropped out of [The University of Connecticut Health Center](https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-university-of-connecticut-health-center/) while creating [Zynga](https://www.linkedin.com/company/zynga/)’s first title, [Zynga](https://www.linkedin.com/company/zynga/) Poker, in 2007.

Before co-founding [Zynga](https://www.linkedin.com/company/zynga/), Justin had always been interested in technology and entrepreneurship. He started experimenting with different business ideas while in college, and eventually landed on the idea of creating social games that could be played on social networking sites like [Myspace](https://www.linkedin.com/company/myspace/) and [Facebook](https://www.linkedin.com/company/facebook/)

He developed a number of small games on social networking sites and quickly found that people were drawn to the games' simple, addictive gameplay.

Waldron served as the company's president from 2007 to 2011 and then left the company after the IPO.

After exiting from Zynga, he founded [Playco](https://www.linkedin.com/company/playco/) which has received over $100 million in funding from Sequoia Capital and other. Playco is creating games that can be played instantly with friends without app downloads. The company's games are available within popular social applications such as Facebook, LINE, Snapchat, Viber, TikTok, Zoom, and others.

Today, Zynga continues to dominate the gaming industry, and Justin's story is a testament to his brilliance and his unwavering confidence in his abilities.

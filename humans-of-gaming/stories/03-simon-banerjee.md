# Simon Banerjee

**Co-Founder · Xansr Technologies**

5 years ago, Simon stepped down from the studio head role at GSN and went to the foothills of the Himalayas.

10 months later he traveled back to the city lights to start his own game studio.

Featuring Simon Banerjee #HumansOfGaming

Simon's journey in gaming starts back in 2003.

He graduated from Delhi University in 2002 and started working for Babel Media, now known as Universal Keyword Studios.

His professional journey started in 2003 when he started working as a Test Engineer at Babel Media.

He started in a testing role and transitioned into a statistical road after 18 months.

Up next, Simon had two offers in hand. One from Gurgaon which was near his home in Delhi and the other from Hyderabad.

This decision would play a pivotal role in his career as he moved to Hyderabad And took a role in game production.

Still uncertain about building a career in this industry, his penchant for games kept him going.

Two years after moving to Hyderabad, he got an opportunity from Mauritius as a Producer where he spent a year.

Simon came back to India in 2008 and joined the dream gaming studio for many - Electronic Arts. This would turn out to be the second pivotal point in his career.

He joined EA as Project Manager in 2008 and would work there for almost a decade.

Simon worked on various games like The Sims Social, Ritual, Monopoly Slots, Wall Series of Poker before that, and Heroes of Dragon Age.

His stint at EA added a lot more than skills to his professional journey.

Simon also moved between producer and designer roles, which allowed him to develop a more well-rounded perspective on game development.

After 8 long years, Simon moved out of EA and joined GSN as studio head.

He led the GSN studios for over a year and then decided to step down and go to the hills on a sabbatical.

Simon loves traveling and the only thing that pulled him back to city life from the hills was the people around him.

After he came back he started building Xansr Technology with NItu Chiring.

They have secured clients like Imangi while staying bootstrapped and profitable for the last 4 years. They have started working on their own game which would come out soon.
